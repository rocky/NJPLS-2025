The first book "How Debuggers Work", I quote from the a review, which you can read here. The prevailing view found in compiler books and debugger books is that these are separate topics that not very much related to one another.

This reminds me of a couple of places where I worked where the department of computer backups was a separate department from the department of computer restores. The backup group was group to work in — the atomosphere was causal and relaxed. The department of restores group was always under constant pressure and never could get its act together. It feels to me, this is the current situation with debuggers for too many compilers.
